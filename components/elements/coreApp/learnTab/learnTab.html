Getting Started

A great place to get started is the Actions Tab.

Here you can define a set of actions that you can use to chat with your users. Actions are predefined messages that you can send your users. In the Actions tab you can do the following:

Create a group, let’s call it , Welcome

        

 

Next let’s create a Text Action let’s call it  Welcome Greeting.

Use this Action to greet your users when they first message you. Assign a Shortcut, something you can remember. Shortcuts are a nifty way for you to send a message quickly (more on this later).

Next let’s have a conversation in the Chat tab.

Here on your left, you will see a panel with a Timeline view (which you can always toggle to a Follower view), in these views you will see the followers who have sent you messages on facebook.

On your right is the Chat interface that includes the Conversation widget and the Action Palette. The Conversation Widget is similar to the facebook messenger app, here's where you exchange messages with your followers.

 

When you receive a message from one of your facebook page followers, you can always respond by sending them a text message.

Alternatively, in the case of our example, you can simply trigger an Action by using a shortcut-  in this case “//greet”. When you type the Action, you will see a toaster pop, tap the paper plane icon or press enter to send.

You can also send an Action  by tapping on the Action in the Action Palette that's to the right of the Chat Widget.

 

Its that simple you are now ready to go, next learn about the different action types.
